<p><p>Balsamic vinegar is categorized into two main types: Traditional Balsamic and Commercial Balsamic. Traditional Balsamic is produced in specific regions of Italy using traditional methods, aged for a minimum of 12 years. It is known for its rich flavor and complex aroma, often used as a gourmet ingredient. Commercial Balsamic, on the other hand, is mass-produced and widely available in supermarkets. It is less expensive and lacks the depth of flavor and quality found in Traditional Balsamic. Both types cater to different market segments based on quality and price preferences.</p></p>
