<p><p>Cosmetic Titanium Dioxide is produced through two main processes: Chloride Process and Sulfate Process. The Chloride Process involves treating titanium ore with chlorine gas to produce pure titanium dioxide, known for its brightness and high refractive index. On the other hand, the Sulfate Process involves treating titanium ore with sulfuric acid to produce titanium dioxide with slightly lower quality. Both processes yield different grades of titanium dioxide that are used in various cosmetic products for their whitening, UV protection, and opacity properties.</p></p>
