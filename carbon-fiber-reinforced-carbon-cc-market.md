<p><p>Carbon Fiber-Reinforced Carbon (C/C) materials are widely used due to their high strength, temperature resistance, and lightweight properties. The market has two main types of production methods: Chemical Vapor Deposition (CVD) and Liquid Impregnation Process. CVD involves depositing carbon fibers onto a carbon matrix through a chemical reaction at high temperatures. On the other hand, the Liquid Impregnation Process involves impregnating preformed carbon fibers with a liquid carbon precursor, followed by heat treatment to create the final C/C material.</p></p>
