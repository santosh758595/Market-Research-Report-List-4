<p><p>Semi-solid flow batteries are categorized into two main types based on the electrolyte used: organic electrolyte and inorganic electrolyte. Organic electrolyte-based flow batteries use organic compounds as the electrolyte solution, providing advantages such as low cost and high energy density. In contrast, inorganic electrolyte-based flow batteries use inorganic materials like sulfur or vanadium, offering benefits such as high stability and long cycle life. Both types of flow batteries are gaining market traction due to their potential for energy storage applications.</p></p>
