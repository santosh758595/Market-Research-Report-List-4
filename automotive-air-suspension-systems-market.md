<p><p>Automotive air suspension systems can be categorized into two main types: manual air suspension and electronic air suspension. Manual air suspension systems require manual adjustment of air pressure in the suspension system to control the ride height and stiffness of the vehicle. On the other hand, electronic air suspension systems use sensors and an electronic control unit to automatically adjust the air pressure in the suspension system based on driving conditions and driver preferences. Both types offer improved ride quality, handling, and comfort compared to traditional suspension systems.</p></p>
