<p><p>Traditional PET-MRI Systems combine positron emission tomography (PET) and magnetic resonance imaging (MRI) technologies for simultaneous anatomical and functional imaging. These systems typically use liquid helium for cooling the superconducting magnets, making them expensive to operate. In contrast, Helium-free PET-MRI Systems utilize alternative cooling methods, eliminating the need for helium and reducing operational costs. This emerging market offers a more cost-effective and sustainable solution for healthcare facilities seeking to invest in advanced imaging technology.</p></p>
