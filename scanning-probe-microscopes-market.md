<p><p>Scanning Probe Microscopes (SPMs) are advanced microscopy techniques that allow for high-resolution imaging and manipulation of surfaces at the nanoscale level. There are three main types of SPMs: Atomic Force Microscopes (AFM) which measure forces between a sharp probe and sample surface, Scanning Tunneling Microscopes (STM) which detect tunneling current between probe and sample, and Near-field Scanning Optical Microscopes (NSOM) which use a small aperture to achieve subwavelength spatial resolution. These techniques have revolutionized the field of nanotechnology and materials science.</p></p>
