<p><p>A thermal fan clutch is a component of a vehicle's cooling system that regulates the operation of the engine cooling fan based on the temperature of the engine. When the engine is cool, the fan clutch disengages and allows the fan to spin at a low speed, conserving energy. As the engine temperature rises, the fan clutch engages and causes the fan to spin faster, increasing airflow over the radiator and helping to cool the engine.</p><p>The global thermal fan clutch market is expected to grow at a CAGR of 4.7% during the forecast period. This growth is driven by an increasing demand for fuel-efficient vehicles, as thermal fan clutches help to improve overall vehicle efficiency by reducing the energy consumption of the cooling system. <p><p>Thermal fan clutches in the market are available in two main types: Standard Rotation and Reverse Rotation. Standard Rotation fan clutches are designed to rotate the same direction as the engine's rotation, and are commonly used in most vehicles. On the other hand, Reverse Rotation fan clutches are designed to rotate in the opposite direction of the engine's rotation, and are typically found in specific makes and models of vehicles. Both types of fan clutches play a crucial role in regulating the engine's temperature by controlling the speed of the cooling fan.</p></p>
