<p><p>Continuous level measurement instruments are used to monitor and control the level of liquids or solids in tanks and vessels. The market for these instruments can be classified into various types based on the technology used. These types include differential pressure, guided wave radar, non-contact radar, ultrasonic, capacitance, and other technologies. Differential pressure instruments measure level by detecting the pressure difference between the bottom of the tank and the liquid surface. Guided wave radar uses radar waves to measure level, while non-contact radar does so without touching the liquid. Ultrasonic instruments use sound waves, capacitance measures level based on electrical properties, and other technologies include laser, nuclear, and magnetic-based sensors.</p></p>
