<p><p>Injectable drug packaging includes ampules, vials, cartridges, and bottles. Ampules are small sealed containers made of glass, vials are small bottles typically made of plastic or glass, cartridges are cylindrical containers with a rubber stopper, and bottles are larger containers typically made of glass or plastic. These different types of packaging are used to store and deliver various injectable drugs such as vaccines, insulin, and other medications. Each type of packaging has specific advantages and is chosen based on the requirements of the drug and its administration.</p></p>
