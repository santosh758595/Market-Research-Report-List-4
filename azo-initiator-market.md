<p><p>Azo-initiators are classified into two main market types based on their solubility in either oil or water. Oil-soluble azo-initiators are predominantly used in oil-based systems, such as coatings and adhesives, where they can easily dissolve and initiate a polymerization reaction. On the other hand, water-soluble azo-initiators are more commonly employed in aqueous systems, such as emulsions and paints, where they can disperse and initiate polymerization in water-based environments. Both types of azo-initiators play a crucial role in various industries where polymerization processes are essential.</p></p>

<p><ul><li>Bulk Polymerization</li><li>Suspension Polymerization</li><li>Solution Polymerization</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Azo-initiators are widely used in various polymerization processes such as bulk, suspension, and solution polymerization. In bulk polymerization, azo-initiators are added directly to the monomer mixture, initiating the reaction to form solid polymer products. In suspension polymerization, they help create polymer particles suspended in a liquid medium. In solution polymerization, azo-initiators are dissolved in a solvent along with the monomers, initiating the polymerization process. These applications play a crucial role in the production of a wide range of polymers.</p></p>
