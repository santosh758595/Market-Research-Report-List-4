<p><p>Pumped Hydroelectric Energy Storage (PHES) is a method of storing energy by using two reservoirs at different elevations. During times of low electricity demand, excess electricity is used to pump water from a lower reservoir to a higher reservoir. When electricity demand is high, the water is released back down to the lower reservoir, passing through turbines which generate electricity.</p><p>The PHES market is expected to grow at a CAGR of 7.4% during the forecast period. The increasing demand for renewable energy sources, coupled with the need for energy storage solutions to compensate for the intermittent nature of sources like solar and wind power, is driving the growth of the PHES market. Additionally, the ability of PHES systems to provide grid stability and support the integration of variable renewable energy sources is also contributing to its market growth.</p><p>Some of the latest trends in the PHES market include the development of advanced software and technology solutions to improve efficiency and operation of PHES systems, as well as the integration of PHES with other renewable energy sources to create hybrid energy systems.
