<p><p>Superconducting Fault Current Limiters (SFCL) are used to protect power systems from damage caused by short circuits. There are four main types of SFCLs on the market: Shielded-Core SFCL, Saturable-Core SFCL, Hybrid Resistive SFCL, and Purely Resistive SFCL. Shielded-Core SFCLs use superconducting materials to shield the core, while Saturable-Core SFCLs use a saturable core to limit fault currents. Hybrid Resistive SFCLs combine superconducting and resistive elements, while Purely Resistive SFCLs rely solely on resistive materials to limit fault currents.</p></p>
