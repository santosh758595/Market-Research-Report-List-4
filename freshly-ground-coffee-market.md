<p><p>Freshly ground coffee is available in three main market types based on the size of the ground beans: rough grinding, medium grinding, and fine grinding. Rough grinding produces large coffee grounds suitable for French press and percolators. Medium grinding creates a versatile size suitable for drip coffee makers and Aeropress. Fine grinding results in very small, powdery grounds ideal for espresso machines. Each market type caters to different brewing methods and preferences, giving consumers a variety of options to choose from based on their tastes and equipment.</p></p>
